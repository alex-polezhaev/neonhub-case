# The model: an AI-native business

NeonHub is a small neon-sign shop. But it's an early, working example of something much bigger: a business where almost the **entire value chain runs on AI and automation**, and the only irreducibly human step is handing a physical product to the customer.

Look at where the work actually goes:

| Step | Who does it | How |
|---|---|---|
| **Make the product photos** | 🤖 AI | Thousands of neon-sign images generated by Google's Gemini image models via the **Batch API (-50% cost)**: no photographer, no product shoots, no studio. |
| **Build the storefront** | 🤖 AI + automation | A static, SEO-engineered site built from the catalog, self-syndicating to search and marketing feeds: no agency, no content team. |
| **Process the orders** | ⚙️ Automation | Orders flow through the same n8n + spreadsheet backbone that runs the catalog. |
| **Make & deliver the goods** | 🧑 Human | Bending the tube, wiring the sign, packing the box, shipping it. |

The catalog that used to need a photographer, a designer, and a content team is now a **prompt library and a batch job**. The storefront that used to need a dev shop is a **static export that builds and indexes itself**. The marketing that used to need an agency is a **set of generated feeds**. Each of those was once a salaried role or an outsourced contract. Here, each is a script.

What's left for the human is the part AI can't (yet) touch: the **atoms**, manufacturing the physical sign and getting it to the door.

## Why this is the shape of things to come

For decades, the cost of *looking like* a real business (a full catalog, a polished storefront, a marketing engine) was a moat. It took people and money. AI is dissolving that moat. A solo founder can now stand up a catalog of thousands of products, a fast SEO storefront, and an order pipeline in the time it used to take just to brief a photographer.

That changes **who** can start a product business and **how small** it can be. The emerging shape looks like NeonHub:

- **Tiny team, wide catalog.** The catalog scales with compute, not headcount.
- **Near-zero marginal cost on the digital side.** Generating the 2,581st product image costs cents; adding it to the storefront costs nothing.
- **The human concentrates on atoms.** Design taste, manufacturing quality, and fulfillment: the things that are still hard, and still matter.

NeonHub already runs this way: **2,580 products and 8,427 generated images across 103 batch jobs**, a storefront that bakes and indexes itself, and a founder whose remaining job is to make the sign and ship it.

It isn't a forecast. It's a working instance of the model. And the model is going to be everywhere.
